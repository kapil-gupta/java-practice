YOUR CORE TRADING RULES
BUY SIGNAL
Stock is above January High
When price clears and holds above the January high, the structure is bullish. This is your long bias. Execute only on proper retest and confirmation — not on the initial break.
SELL SIGNAL
Stock is below January Low
When price breaks and holds below the January low, the structure is bearish. This is your short bias. Do not fight it. Do not buy dips in a structural downtrend.
WEEKLY RSI ≥ 85
Extreme Overbought — Exit & Avoid 2 Years
If Weekly RSI crosses 85 on any stock or sector — sell it immediately. Do not buy that instrument for the next 2 years. This is a hard rule. No exceptions. Extreme RSI = extreme risk.
WEEKLY RSI ≤ 25
Extreme Oversold — Look for Longs
Weekly RSI at or below 25 signals extreme oversold conditions. Begin looking for buy setups with confluence from structure, liquidity sweeps, and demand zones. Do not catch a falling knife — wait for confirmation.

THE OPENING RANGE RULE
"Stop losing money in the first 30 minutes. Most traders jump in too early and get chopped up."
— Your own notes
The Problem
First 30 Minutes Is a Trap
The opening 30 minutes is maximum noise, maximum manipulation, and minimum signal. Market makers are filling orders, stop hunts are happening, and algos are testing both sides. Entering here is gambling — not trading.
The Protocol — 4 Steps
Opening Range Breakout + Retest
Mark the HIGH and LOW of the first 15–30 minutes (the Opening Range)
Wait for price to break OUT of that range cleanly — above high or below low
Wait for price to RETEST that broken level and hold (old resistance = new support)
THEN enter when price continues in the breakout direction
No break + retest = No trade. Stay cash. Protect capital.
Your Stop Loss
The opening range you just marked IS your stop loss level.
If the range isn't broken cleanly — don't trade that day. Patience pays. Chasing chop doesn't. The key isn't taking more trades. It's avoiding the bad ones.

PRICE ACTION PRINCIPLES
"No matter what tools or indicators you use for trading, your decision making must always be based on price action (supply/demand). This will mostly keep you on the right side."
— Your notes
Candle Close = Intent
Price doesn't move randomly.
The close of a candle decides the delivery of the next. A weak close = indecision. A strong close = intent. Intent tells you whether price wants to Continue, Displace, or Retrace. The next candle executes that intent.
Wicks Are Not Noise
Wicks are the damage.
Upper wick → buy-side liquidity swept, shorts built. Lower wick → sell-side liquidity taken, accumulation at discount. Retail sees fake breakouts. Smart Money sees positions filled. Bodies show acceptance. Wicks show manipulation.
Timeframe Rule
Analyse on H1/H4. Enter on 5M/15M.
Do your analysis on the 1H or 4H chart, then take your entry on the 5M or 15M. This eliminates noise on entry while keeping your bias clean from the higher timeframe. Dropping to lower timeframes without HTF context = more doubt, more mistakes.
Noise Warning
Switch up when confused.
If you cannot read the chart — it's the noise, not you. Switch to a higher timeframe. Clarity lives on higher timeframes. Confusion lives on lower ones. Master one setup. One timeframe. One instrument. Simple beats exciting. Every time.
4H Liquidity Sweep → 15M Confirmation
The Core Entry Framework (ICT/SMC)
1. Identify a 4H Liquidity Sweep — price moves sharply to take stops at highs/lows before reversing.

2. Drop to 15M. Wait for MSS (Market Structure Shift) — price breaks a recent high/low signalling a trend change.

3. Confirm with CISD (Change In State of Delivery) — a specific MSS variant indicating a bias flip (bullish → bearish or vice versa).

4. Enter at premium/discount zone. Use proper risk management. Backtest before deploying live.

06
Institutions trade imbalances, positioning, and risk — not indicators. Indicators are for confirmation, never for decision-making. Price is the primary input.
07

08

09
Most "breakouts" exist only to create liquidity. The real move starts after traders are stopped out. Don't be the stop. Wait for the sweep, then trade the real move.
10
Order Blocks are where the move was born. Price returns to OBs to rebalance unfilled institutional orders. Note it. Print it. Learn it. This is why price snaps back before exploding.

SMC won't make you profitable. Discipline, patience, and risk management will.
SMC just shows you WHERE to execute. The HOW is still your psychology and discipline.
If your SMC setup needs hope — it's not SMC. It's gambling.

06
EXECUTION RULES
🎯
High Conviction Trade? Enter — Don't Wait for "Perfect"
When you find an A+ setup, just enter. A+ setups move fast — waiting for the perfect entry means missing the trade. Enter with 20–25% of standard size if current price is away from your ideal level, and add the rest at your desired entry. Keep total risk the same as always. Best of both worlds.
🚫
Missed Trade? Forget It. Move On.
Missing one trade won't hurt you because it didn't happen. But taking a revenge trade because you missed one can seriously damage your account. If a trade is missed — forget it. Focus on the next one. There is always another setup.
📐
The 4 Rules of Consistent Execution
1. Clearly defined setup — entry and exits known before you enter.
2. Rule-based — what to trade, when to trade, no improvisation.
3. Emotionless — robotic execution, no feelings involved.
4. Defined risk management — risk/reward is set before entry, always.
Ignore any one of these and trading becomes very hard.
🧘
After a Loss → Step Away. Always.
Protect capital. Protect mindset. The market will always be there. Your real edge isn't the setup. It's your ability to NOT trade when your ego is triggered. Smart Money doesn't revenge trade. When price violates your POI — that's information, not an insult. Losses are data. Ego turns them into drawdown.
📅
Weekend Homework Is Not Optional
Successful traders plan the week ahead by doing their homework over the weekend. Unsuccessful traders get into the week without a plan and pick random trades, only to lose. Mark your key levels, identify your setups, define your bias on Sunday — before the week starts.
⏸
Mastering Sitting on Your Hands
Mastering the art of sitting on your hands when there's no trade is a game changer. It can save you more money than constantly chasing trades outside your plan. No setup = sit. Waiting IS trading.
